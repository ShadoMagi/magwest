Volunteer Agreement

This Volunteer Agreement has two sections; one is legal and the other is cultural. In the state of California, we're required by law to explain the exchange of perks for volunteer hours. Please note that "Provided Services" is where we'd typically say "volunteer".

Beyond that, it's important to us that you understand volunteer expectations, as well as what you can expect from MAGFest. Read on!

MAGFest Legal Volunteer Agreement

This Volunteer Agreement (the "Agreement") is entered into by and between MAGFest, Inc. (the "Company"), and {{ attendee.full_name }} ("Volunteer"), and is effective as of {{ now()|datetime("%m/%d, %Y") }} (the "Effective Date").

In consideration of the mutual promises contained herein and other good and sufficient consideration, the receipt and adequacy of which is hereby acknowledged, the parties hereby agree as follows:

Services. Volunteer agrees that he/she will provide the following services, ("Services"), to the Company:
(a) attend the Company's event ("{{ c.EVENT_NAME }}") Volunteer training;
(b) assist the Company in hosting {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} and assist {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} patrons;
(c) preparation and cleaning of the {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} venue; and
(d) any other tasks requested by the Company.

Volunteer agrees that he/she would like to provide the Services without compensation and wishes to contribute his/her time. All costs and expenses incurred by Volunteer in providing the Services shall be the responsibility of Volunteer, unless approved in writing in advance by the Company.

Perks. Volunteer shall receive the following Perks determined by the amount of Services provided by the Volunteer::
(a) a Volunteer that achieves eight (8) hours of Services will be entitled to a {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} t-shirt;
(b) a Volunteer that achieves sixteen (16) hours of Services will be entitled to access to meals provided by the Company;
(c) a Volunteer that achieves twenty-four (24) hours of Services will be entitled to reimbursement of the amount, if any, Volunteer paid for Volunteer's {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} badge; and
(d) a Volunteer that achieves thirty (30) hours of Services at this {{ c.EVENT_NAME }} AND provided Services at one of the Company's previous events will be entitled to shared space in one of the Company's staff hotel rooms for this {{ c.EVENT_NAME }}.
Other than the aforesaid amounts Volunteer acknowledges and agrees he/she is not entitled to any compensation of any type from {{ c.EVENT_NAME }}.

Term. Volunteer's Services will commence on the Effective Date and will remain in effect until {{ agreement_end_date|datetime("%m/%d/%Y") }} unless earlier terminated by the Company, for any reason or no reason, effective immediately upon written notice to Volunteer.

Acknowledgement. Volunteer acknowledges and agrees that:
(a) the Services will be performed faithfully and to the best of his/her ability;
(b) the Services will be performed in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations;
(c) neither the Services nor the Work Product will infringe the rights of any third party or violate any law; and
(d) Volunteer has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement and to provide the Services in accordance with this Agreement. The Company acknowledges and agrees that it has full power and authority to enter into this Agreement.

Miscellaneous. This Agreement and all performance under this Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of California, without regard to conflict of law principles. In any dispute relating to this Agreement, the parties hereto admit venue and submit themselves to the exclusive jurisdiction of the state or federal courts located in Los Angeles County, California. Volunteer's remedy, if any, for any breach of this Agreement shall be solely in damages, and Volunteer shall not be entitled to equitable relief by way of injunction or otherwise. In the event any provision of this Agreement is determined to be invalid, prohibited or unenforceable by a court or other body of competent jurisdiction, this Agreement shall be construed as if such provision has been more narrowly drawn so as not to be invalid, prohibited or unenforceable. No waiver of any provision of this Agreement will be effective unless it is in writing and signed by both parties, and no such waiver will result in the waiver of any other provision of this Agreement. Failure of either party at any time to enforce any of the provisions of this Agreement will not be construed as a waiver of such provisions or in any way affect the validity of this Agreement or parts thereof. In performing the Services, Volunteer will act and represent itself as an independent volunteer and not as an employee or agent of the Company. Volunteer has no authority to obligate the Company by contract or otherwise. Volunteer may not assign any rights or obligations under this Agreement without the advance written consent of the Company, which may be withheld in its sole discretion. The Company may assign its rights or obligations under this Agreement in its sole discretion. This Agreement is the parties' final, exclusive and complete understanding and agreement, and supersedes all prior and contemporaneous understandings and agreements relating to the subject matter of this Agreement. This Agreement may be amended only by a writing signed by both parties. This Agreement may be executed in counterparts which, when taken together, will constitute one Agreement.

MAGFest Community Volunteer Agreement

MAGFest depends on committed volunteers to make our events happen. We could not do this without you and we are so grateful for all you do and the community you have helped to create. To ensure that staff, volunteers, attendees, and guests have the best experience possible, we have developed the following agreement between volunteers and MAGFest, Inc.

MAGFest, Inc. agrees to:

  • Be forthcoming with information as it pertains to event logistics and changes in policy.
  • Serve as the liaison between volunteers and employees when needed.
  • Provide a brief description of duties for positions open to volunteers.
  • Train and support volunteers where needed.
  • Provide volunteers with working conditions equal to those of paid employees doing similar work, including space, equipment and supplies.
  • Provide volunteers the opportunity to ask questions, communicate openly, and share any concerns.
  • Be responsible for the coordination, support, and termination (if necessary) of all volunteers.

The Volunteer agrees to:

  • Meet the commitment required of chosen position. This includes being aware of pre-event duties or deadlines and completing them accordingly.
  • Become thoroughly familiar with MAGFest's Event Code of Conduct, Volunteer Guidelines and Policies, and Communications Code of Conduct.
  • Carry out duties in alignment with our Core Values.
  • Work all shifts that they have signed up for, being present and prepared to work on time. If unable to work as scheduled, they will notify their department supervisor.
  • Seek the assistance of their immediate supervisor or DH in any situation requiring special guidance.
  • Provide feedback and suggestions to STOPS in order to increase the effectiveness of the program.
  • Accept the right of MAGFest, Inc. to dismiss any volunteer for poor performance, including poor attendance.
  • Notify MAGFest about any resignation, request for a leave of absence, or change of volunteer commitment.

Performance Problems and Disciplinary Actions

In general, volunteers can receive verbal or written warnings of unacceptable behavior or performance and are allowed time and support to improve.

MAGFest supports an atmosphere of open discussion of issues between volunteers, staff, and employees. Should a volunteer take exception to a disciplinary action, they may discuss it with the head(s) of STOPS for their event. In the case that further arbitration is needed, the Director of Volunteer Infrastructure will have final say on disciplinary actions regarding volunteers.

In instances of gross misconduct, warnings may not be possible or desirable for the overall interests of MAGFest, event attendees, or the community. Engaging in any of the following behaviors or actions will not be tolerated and may result in immediate termination of duties:

  • Physical assault or threat of violence to anyone involved with the event.
  • Theft (equipment, supplies, money).
  • Possession or use of a weapon.
  • Racial, sexual, or other harassment of anyone involved with the event.
  • Reckless or deliberate action that endangers the physical or psychological well-being of another person involved with the event.
  • Reckless or deliberate action that damages MAGFest property or property belonging to MAGFest partners.
  • Reckless or deliberate action which damages the reputation of MAGFest.
